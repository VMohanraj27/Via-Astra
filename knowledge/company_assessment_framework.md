# Company Research & Assessment Framework

This framework defines the structured criteria used to evaluate a company when considering a job opportunity.  
The research process primarily relies on **structured web intelligence gathered using Tavily**.

The evaluation prioritizes **business alignment first**, followed by **technology exposure, growth opportunities, work environment, and compensation viability**.

---

# 1. Core Offerings & Services

Understand the primary business offerings of the company and the markets it serves.

Research should identify:

- Core products or services
- Primary revenue streams
- Major customer segments
- Industry verticals served
- Key differentiators in the market
- Strategic positioning relative to competitors

This step establishes **whether the company’s business domain aligns with long-term career interests**.

---

# 2. AI & Data Footprint

Evaluate the company’s presence and investments in Artificial Intelligence and Data technologies.

Key considerations:

- Historical AI and machine learning initiatives
- Current AI-driven products or internal systems
- Planned AI or automation initiatives in the company roadmap
- Research collaborations, partnerships, or innovation labs
- Investments in generative AI, data platforms, or advanced analytics
- Adoption of data-driven decision-making practices

This helps determine **how central AI/ML is to the company’s future strategy**.

---

# 3. Technology Stack & Engineering Tools

Identify the technologies used by engineering teams and the maturity of the technical ecosystem.

Areas to investigate:

- Backend technologies and software architecture
- Cloud platforms and infrastructure (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- Data engineering platforms and pipelines
- MLOps ecosystems and model deployment pipelines
- AI/ML frameworks and tooling
- CI/CD pipelines and developer productivity tooling

This assessment determines **technical exposure and relevance to modern engineering practices**.

---

# 4. Learning & Upskilling Opportunities

Assess how the company supports employee learning and professional development.

Key aspects:

- Internal training programs and engineering academies
- Certifications or sponsored learning initiatives
- Access to internal knowledge-sharing platforms
- Opportunities to work on real customer implementations
- Exposure to production-grade systems and large-scale deployments
- Participation in innovation initiatives or hackathons

This section evaluates **long-term skill development potential**.

---

# 5. Company Culture

Investigate the company’s culture through multiple sources.

Sources to analyze:

- Company website and culture pages
- Engineering blogs or leadership interviews
- Public employee reviews
- Leadership messaging and internal values

Important considerations:

- Collaboration style and engineering autonomy
- Work-life balance expectations
- Leadership philosophy
- Transparency and communication practices
- Employee growth opportunities

**Note:** Public review platforms may contain biased or unverified opinions, so insights should be interpreted cautiously.

---

# 6. Work Structure & Engagement Model

Understand the operational model and day-to-day working environment.

Areas to research:

- Work hours and shift expectations
- Remote, hybrid, or on-site work models
- Customer engagement structures
- Global team collaboration
- Regions or markets where the company operates
- Delivery models (product-driven vs service-driven)

This helps determine **practical working conditions and lifestyle impact**.

---

# 7. Role-Based Research

Conduct targeted research on the specific job role being applied for.

Key questions:

- How does the role contribute to the company’s business outcomes?
- What teams or products does the role support?
- What technologies and responsibilities are involved?
- What level of ownership and decision-making authority exists?
- What career growth trajectory does the role provide?

This section ensures **the position itself aligns with career goals**.

---

# 8. Competitive Salary Assessment

Evaluate whether the company can realistically meet the candidate’s expected compensation.

Consider:

- Salary benchmarks for the role and experience level
- Compensation trends within the company
- Market comparisons for similar roles
- Region-specific salary adjustments
- Total compensation including bonuses, stock options, and benefits
